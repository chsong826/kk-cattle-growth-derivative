🐂 Kedah-Kelantan Cattle Study & Data Origin

This biological simulation models the growth kinetics of Kedah-Kelantan (KK) cattle, an indigenous Malaysian Bos indicus breed widely reared throughout northern Peninsular Malaysia. KK cattle are highly adapted to tropical heat, resistant to local diseases, and capable of surviving on low-quality forage systems.

Growth data and mature weight parameters were adapted from Malaysian cattle production studies conducted by Universiti Putra Malaysia (UPM), MARDI, and local beef industry references. Mature KK cattle generally reach approximately 220–312 kg body weight depending on sex and management system.

The function f(x) calculates the cumulative body weight of the cattle at age x (months), while the derivative f'(x) calculates the instantaneous growth rate in kg/month. The derivative curve allows farmers to identify the biologically optimal fattening stage for beef production.

Body Weight Function f(x)
f(x) = A × e-e-B(x - x*)

Calculates total cattle body weight (kg) at age x.

Weight Gain Derivative f'(x)
f'(x) = A × B × e-B(x - x*) - e-B(x - x*)

Calculates exact monthly growth rate (kg/month).

📊 Model Parameters
• x: Cattle Age (months)
• x*: Peak Growth Inflection Age
• A: Mature Body Weight (kg)
• B: Real Growth Velocity Coefficient
• k: Genetic Growth Coefficient
• η (eta): Feeding & Environmental Efficiency
